Bunkie



Overview


Project
Class Project (Team of 3)
Scope
Product & UX Design
Tool
Figma

As second and third year college students, we understand the frustration that comes with finding off-campus housing and rommates, especially in New York. We wanted to design a platform that eases that process, while also ensuring user's safety when finding rentals. This project is focused on user research and wireframing.


Problem


There are a couple of apps in the market to help find housing and roommates, but none specifically aimed for college students.


Objective

  • Help college students find ideal roommates and rooms
  • Make roommate/room searching process safe and less time-consuming

To achieve these objectives, we will:

  • Provide a method to matching potential roommates by their traits or attributes
  • Provide an engaging and intriguing mobile interface that works for Millennials
  • Develop a background check system to ensure the safety
  • Provide customer care and 24/7 support

Customer Segmentation

User Type Host/Tenant
Age 18-24
Gender All
Occupation College Student (Undergraduate and Graduate)
Location New York City (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island)

There are two segments of our customers: hosts and tenants. Users are divided up to those groups based on whether they have a room(s) to share or is looking for a room to stay. The former group of people are called hosts, and the latter are the tenants. Both the host and tenant are college students, but they have different needs. Hosts only focus on roommate hunting, while tenants consider both rooms and roommates.

Findings


To reach out to our objectives, we distributed a survey and sent it to our friends and acquaintances who are current college students in NYC. They are room/roommate seekers or have experiences in room/roommate hunting. Here is what we have learned from the 33 respondents:


42.4% feel unsafe about finding roommates in NYC.
90.9% have no experience in using services or websites to find a roommate.


Roommate matching mobile services is not popular among college students in NYC currently. This situation indicates that we have no strong competitors and leaves us an opportunity to be the first service that attracts those potential customers.

Pain Points

After doing competitive analysis and user interview, We identified the key pain points, which are:

01. Finding roommates/rooms are time consuming

02. Potential tenants don't know where to look for rooms/roommates

03. There are lots of ads that are scams
